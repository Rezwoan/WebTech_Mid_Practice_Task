Korail slum fire in Dhaka leaves thousands homeless

Published: November 26, 2025

A massive fire tore through the Korail slum in Dhaka on Tuesday evening, destroying rows of tin-roofed shanties and leaving thousands of residents without shelter. The blaze ripped through one of the capital's largest informal settlements, wedged between the affluent Gulshan and Banani neighbourhoods, sending flames and smoke high into the night sky.

Fire officials say at least 19 fire engines battled the blaze for hours but faced heavy traffic and narrow lanes that forced crews to drag hoses by hand deep into the settlement. Early reports indicate that more than a thousand homes were damaged or destroyed, though authorities have not reported any deaths. Many of the families living in Korail are low‑income workers and climate migrants now sifting through the ashes for anything they can save.

Ousted ex-PM Sheikh Hasina handed 21-year prison sentence

Published: November 27, 2025

A Dhaka special court has sentenced ousted former prime minister Sheikh Hasina to 21 years in prison in three corruption cases over plots of government land in the Purbachal New Town project. The judge ordered three separate seven‑year terms to be served consecutively, saying Hasina and her family abused state power to obtain plots they were not eligible for.

Her son Sajeeb Wazed and daughter Saima Wazed each received five‑year sentences in one of the cases. All three verdicts were delivered in absentia as Hasina has been living in exile in India since being forced from office last year. Earlier in November she was also sentenced to death in a separate trial over a deadly crackdown on a student‑led uprising, a process her party and human rights groups say lacks basic guarantees of fairness.

Dhaka ranked second most polluted city in the world

Published: November 24, 2025

Dhaka has been ranked the world's second most polluted city in a real‑time global index, with an Air Quality Index reading around 225 and levels stuck in the “very unhealthy” range. Monitoring stations across neighbourhoods such as Eastern Housing, Kalyanpur and Goran all reported concentrations well above safe limits as haze continued to blanket the capital.

The air‑quality platform IQAir has urged residents to wear masks outdoors, avoid exercise in the open air and keep windows closed when possible. Public health experts warn that long‑term exposure to this level of pollution increases the risk of heart and lung disease, particularly for children, older adults and outdoor workers.
