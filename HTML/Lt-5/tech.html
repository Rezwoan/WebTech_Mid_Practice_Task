Bangladesh tech firms seek partners at DigiTech ASEAN 2025

Published: November 2025

A delegation of Bangladeshi technology companies has taken part in the DigiTech ASEAN 2025 exhibition in Thailand, looking to expand exports of software and IT‑enabled services. The group, coordinated by industry association BASIS, met potential clients and partners from across Southeast Asia, one of the region's fastest‑growing digital markets.

Company representatives say the event offered a chance to showcase Bangladeshi expertise in cloud services, cybersecurity, business process outsourcing and fintech, while officials highlighted new government incentives aimed at boosting the country's ICT exports over the coming years.

Draft digital transformation roadmap outlines “Bangladesh 2.0” vision

Published: February 16, 2025

Bangladesh's ICT Division, working with the United Nations Development Programme, has released a draft of the National Digital Transformation Strategy. The document lays out a multi‑year plan to modernise digital public services, strengthen cybersecurity and expand the country's digital economy between 2025 and 2030.

The roadmap emphasises building secure digital ID and payment systems, supporting startups, and improving connectivity in rural areas. Officials say the goal is to create an inclusive digital ecosystem that supports both citizens and businesses on the path to a “Smart Bangladesh” by 2041.

Bangladesh Startup Connect 2025 highlights growing ecosystem

Published: May 4, 2025

A new report titled “Bangladesh Startup Connect 2025” paints a positive picture of the country's startup scene, noting rapid growth in sectors such as fintech, logistics, healthtech and e‑commerce. Analysts say Bangladesh is on track to become a trillion‑dollar economy by 2030, with young entrepreneurs playing a central role.

The study calls for more early‑stage funding, clearer regulations and stronger links between local founders and international investors. It also stresses the importance of talent development so that startups can scale regionally rather than focusing only on the domestic market.
